Jollof Rice

Home

Jollof Rice is one of the most iconic West African dishes, known for its smoky flavor, vibrant red color, and rich tomato base. It’s made by cooking rice in a flavorful tomato and pepper sauce, often served with fried plantains, salad, or meat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long grain parboiled rice
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 large onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Bay leaves (optional)
  • Water or chicken stock

Steps

  1. Blend the tomatoes, peppers, and half of the onion into a smooth mixture.
  2. In a pot, heat oil and sauté the sliced onions. Add tomato paste and fry for 2–3 minutes.
  3. Add the blended mixture and cook until it thickens and the oil rises to the top.
  4. Season with thyme, curry, seasoning cubes, and salt. Add bay leaves if using.
  5. Pour in washed rice and stir to coat in the sauce.
  6. Add water or stock to just cover the rice, reduce heat, cover with foil and lid.
  7. Cook on low heat until the rice is soft and liquid is absorbed.
  8. Fluff and serve with fried plantains or protein of choice.
