Semo and Efo Riro is a beloved Nigerian dish that brings together the smooth, satisfying texture of semolina swallow with the rich, spicy flavors of Efo Riro — a vibrant spinach stew. Efo Riro, which means "stirred leafy vegetable" in Yoruba, is typically made with spinach or fluted pumpkin leaves (ugu), simmered with tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, and a blend of traditional spices. It often includes assorted meats, fish, and sometimes stockfish, giving it a deep, smoky flavor. This dish is a staple in many Nigerian households and is enjoyed both for everyday meals and on special occasions. The semo (semolina) serves as the perfect neutral base, used to scoop up generous portions of the flavorful stew by hand — a satisfying, wholesome way to enjoy a truly African culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 bunches of fresh spinach (or 1 pack of frozen spinach)
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2–3 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 large onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 2 cooking spoons of palm oil (or mix with vegetable oil)
  • Assorted meats (beef, tripe, cow skin, or goat meat)
  • Smoked fish or stockfish (optional)
  • 1–2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2–3 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • Locust beans (iru) – optional
  • Water (as needed)

    • Semo

    • 2 cups of semolina flour
    • 3–4 cups of water

    Steps

    1. Wash and cut your meats, then boil them with onions, seasoning cubes, and salt until fully cooked. Set aside.
    2. Blend the red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, tomatoes, and half of the onion into a smooth mixture.
    3. In a large pot, heat palm oil. Add the sliced onions and locust beans (if using), then sauté for a few minutes.
    4. Pour in the blended pepper mix and cook on medium heat until most of the water evaporates and the sauce thickens.
    5. Add the boiled meats and some of the stock into the sauce. Stir well and let it simmer for 5–10 minutes.
    6. Add ground crayfish, smoked fish (if using), seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir and allow it to simmer for another 5 minutes.
    7. Add the spinach last and stir it into the sauce. Cook for about 3–5 minutes until the leaves are soft and everything is well mixed.
    8. Turn off the heat and set aside. Efo Riro is ready!
    9. To make semo, bring water to a boil in a pot.
    10. Gradually pour in semolina flour while stirring continuously with a wooden spatula to prevent lumps.
    11. Keep stirring and folding until it thickens and forms a smooth, stretchy dough.
    12. Serve hot with the Efo Riro stew.
