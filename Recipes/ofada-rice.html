Ofada Rice

Home

Ofada Rice is a locally grown Nigerian rice, known for its unique aroma, unpolished grains, and slightly sticky texture. It’s traditionally served with Ayamase (Ofada sauce), a rich green pepper sauce loaded with assorted meats, locust beans, and palm oil

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Ofada rice
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Assorted meats (beef, tripe, cow skin)
  • 1 cup green bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 1 large onion
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish
  • Locust beans (iru)
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Rinse Ofada rice thoroughly with water to remove stones or dirt. Boil with water and a pinch of salt until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Blend the green peppers, scotch bonnet, and onions coarsely.
  3. Boil assorted meats with seasoning cubes and onions until fully cooked.
  4. Heat palm oil in a pot until it begins to smoke (bleach it carefully), then allow it to cool slightly.
  5. Add the blended pepper mix and cook until the water evaporates and it thickens.
  6. Stir in locust beans, crayfish, seasoning, salt, and the cooked meats. Let it simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Serve the Ofada rice hot, topped generously with the spicy sauce.
